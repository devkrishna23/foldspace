About Foldspace

Software should bend to you, not the other way around

We build tools that give everyone the power to shape the software they use every day, without writing a line of code.

Foldspace began with a simple frustration: the tools people use at work are rigid, siloed, and rarely fit how any individual team actually operates. Knowledge ends up scattered across a wiki, a docs app, a project tracker, and a dozen chat threads. Our founders believed the answer was not another single-purpose app, but a flexible canvas that any team could shape into exactly the workspace they needed.

Our mission

We want to make software toolmaking ubiquitous. Foldspace is built from a small set of composable pieces, blocks, pages, and databases, that anyone can arrange into docs, wikis, project trackers, CRMs, or anything else. The goal is to give non-engineers the kind of leverage that used to require custom software.

Who we build for

Foldspace is used by more than 100 million people: students organizing their lives, founders running lean startups, and large companies standardizing how thousands of employees share knowledge. What they have in common is a desire for fewer, more connected tools, and a workspace that adapts to them as they grow.

Security and trust

We treat your workspace with the care it deserves.

  • Encryption in transit and at rest across the platform.
  • SAML single sign-on and granular permissions, with private team spaces on Business and above.
  • Admin controls and audit logs for visibility into how your workspace is used.
  • User provisioning (SCIM) and advanced security on Enterprise.
  • Your data is yours. Workspace content is not used to train AI models, and you can export at any time.

We are still early in the journey toward software that everyone can shape. But every day, more teams replace a tangle of disconnected apps with one workspace that finally fits the way they work.
