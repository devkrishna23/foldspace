Getting started with databases in Foldspace

6 min read · Foldspace team

Databases are what make Foldspace more than a notes app. A database is just a collection of pages with shared properties, and once it clicks, you will start seeing everything as one.

The core idea

Every row in a database is a full page you can open and write in. The columns are properties: text, dates, selects, people, relations to other databases, and more. The same data can be shown as a table, board, calendar, timeline, list, or gallery, so you pick the view that fits the moment.

A simple first build

  • Create a Tasks database with properties for Status (select), Due date, and Assignee (person).
  • Add a Board view grouped by Status to work like a kanban board.
  • Add a Calendar view of the same data to see deadlines.
  • Relate it to a Projects database so each task links to its parent project.

That single relation, tasks linked to projects, is the moment most people realize why Foldspace connects work in a way separate apps cannot.

Personal productivity

How to build a second brain in Foldspace

5 min read · Foldspace team

A second brain is a trusted place to capture everything you want to remember, so your actual brain can focus on thinking instead of storing. Foldspace is a natural home for one.

A lightweight system

  • Capture quickly into a single Inbox database. Do not organize yet, just get it out of your head.
  • Organize weekly by tagging notes into a few areas: Projects, Areas, Resources, and Archive.
  • Connect related notes with relations, so ideas link to the projects they feed.
  • Retrieve with filtered views and Foldspace AI, which can answer questions across everything you have saved.

The trick is to keep capture frictionless and let structure emerge over time, rather than designing the perfect system on day one.

Migrating

Migrating a team wiki from Confluence to Foldspace

4 min read · Foldspace team

Moving your team wiki sounds daunting, but it is usually a single afternoon. Here is the approach that keeps nothing behind.

The steps

  • Import. Use Foldspace’s Confluence importer to bring pages across with their hierarchy intact.
  • Reorganize into one home. Create a top-level Team Wiki page and nest your spaces under it, so there is a single front door.
  • Assign owners and verify. Mark key pages as verified and set an owner, so your team trusts what is current.
  • Add a database of docs. Turn your list of pages into a database with properties like Owner, Status, and Last reviewed, so the wiki stays healthy.

Once it is in Foldspace, your wiki sits right next to your projects and docs, and Foldspace AI can answer questions straight from it.
